# IBM HR Employee Attrition & Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce performance using the IBM HR dataset.

The goal is to understand which factors are most strongly associated with employees leaving the company and to turn this information into meaningful insights for HR analysis.

The analysis explores different aspects of the workforce, including age, education level, department, and job role, along with the distribution of attrition. It also examines key factors such as monthly income, overtime, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and years at the company.

The project highlights the main relationships within the HR data, making it easier to identify the factors that most influence employee turnover and the areas where the company could take action.

The analysis and visualizations were created using SQL for data processing and Power BI for building the interactive dashboard.

## Tools Used

- SQL
- Power BI

## Dashboard Structure

### Page 1 - HR Overview / KPI Dashboard

This page provides a general overview of employees and attrition within the company. It presents key indicators such as total employees, attrition rate, average age, average monthly income, and average years at the company, summarizing the overall HR situation.

In addition to these indicators, it shows the distribution of employees by department and job role, together with a general view of attrition, with the goal of providing a quick understanding of the company structure and identifying where employee loss is most concentrated.

### Page 2 - Employee Demographics

This page analyzes the composition of employees based on age, gender, education level, and marital status. The goal is to understand how employees are distributed within the company and which groups are most represented. These insights help build a clearer picture of the company's workforce.

### Page 3 - Attrition Drivers

This page analyzes some of the main factors associated with employee attrition. The analysis examines elements such as overtime, monthly income, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and years at the company.

The goal is to identify patterns and differences between employees who left the company and those who stayed, highlighting factors that may influence employee retention.
